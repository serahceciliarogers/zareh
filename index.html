ABOUT PANCHLA

Located just about 20 kilometers from the bustling city of Kolkata,
Panchla is a vibrant community development block in the Howrah district.
It's name, literally translating to "Five Villages," hints at its historical roots as a cluster
of settlements that grew together into a thriving hub of culture and commerce.
While the region is framed by the serene alluvial plains of the Damodar and Hooghly
rivers, Panchla's true identity lies in its people.
It is a land where agricultural heritage meets industrial skill a place where the
skyline is dotted with both green fields and the busy workshops of master artisans.
Today, Panchla stands as a symbol of rural resilience and the timeless beauty of
Bengal's traditional crafts.

OUR COMMUNITY

The Fabric of Our Community

In the heart of Panchla, Zari Zardosi is more than a trade, it is a shared language.
Across every doorstep, the rhythmic pull of the needle bridges generations, where
elders pass down sacred patterns to the young, and gender gives way to the shared
pursuit of beauty.
When a community gathers around the Khaat (embroidery frame), the distinction
between neighbor and kin disappears. Every stitch is a conversation, every finished
piece a collective triumph. Here, craftsmanship doesn't just create luxury; it weaves
the very people of Panchla into a single, unbreakable thread of heritage.
